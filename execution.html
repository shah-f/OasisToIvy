Launch Path

Three stages from technical review to production.

Stage 1

Discover

Weeks 0–2

Payment flow mapping Success criteria definition
  • Align on fraud problems, payment flows, and escalation logic
  • Review data inputs, latency expectations, and operational requirements
  • Set pilot metrics around fraud capture, false positives, and analyst lift
Stage 2

Validate

Weeks 3–8

Model calibration Sandbox or pilot evaluation
  • Run model tuning for the institution’s payment environment
  • Test decision quality, analyst usability, and false-positive behavior
  • Prepare production-readiness checkpoints with fraud and technology teams
Stage 3

Launch and Scale

Weeks 8–12+

Production rollout Monitoring and optimization
  • Move from pilot to live transaction coverage
  • Support governance, monitoring, and operational tuning
  • Expand to additional payment flows or business units as needed
Regional Deployment Readiness

Strongest initial fit in UK and Singapore payment environments.

UK

Faster Payments and APP fraud focus

  • Relevant for institutions exposed to APP reimbursement pressure
  • No dedicated FCA authorization is typically required for fraud-detection SaaS
  • Common customer expectations include ISO 27001, PCI DSS, and SOC 2 Type II readiness
  • UK-hosted cloud patterns and UK GDPR alignment support enterprise deployment discussions
  • Supports explainable outputs for fraud, operations, and governance teams
  • Good fit for challenger banks, mid-tier PSPs, and broader banking programs
  • Deployment narrative aligns with high urgency and direct buyer pain
Singapore

Low-friction rollout with strong regional leverage

  • English-language operations simplify rollout and stakeholder coordination
  • Sandbox-friendly posture fits pilot-led deployment conversations
  • Relevant for domestic banks and APAC-facing payment teams
  • Good platform for broader regional evaluation after initial validation
Engagement Models

Two commercial paths depending on customer size and buying motion.

Fast-track

Pilot-led path for digital banks and PSPs

  • Best for challenger banks, fintechs, and mid-tier PSPs
  • Focused on faster evaluation and direct business-case validation
  • Typical motion: API review, sandbox trial, production rollout
  • Useful when fraud and ops leaders want rapid proof of value
Enterprise

Structured rollout for larger institutions

  • Best for Tier 1 banks, processors, and complex enterprise environments
  • 12–24 month buying cycles are realistic for large-bank procurement
  • Supports longer governance, procurement, and compliance cycles
  • Can include systems integrator or delivery-partner support
  • Designed for customers that need stronger implementation assurance
100-Day View

What a practical implementation sprint can look like.

Days 1–25 Align payment flows, review data inputs, confirm fraud priorities, and define pilot success criteria.
Days 26–50 Configure the initial environment, calibrate model behavior, and validate technical integration points.
Days 51–75 Run sandbox or pilot scenarios, assess alert quality, and refine thresholds with fraud and operations teams.
Days 76–100 Prepare production go-live, finalize monitoring, and establish the operating rhythm for post-launch optimization.
Implementation principle

The fastest route to value is not “everything at once.” It is a tightly scoped payment use case, clear operating metrics, and a deployment path that both fraud and technology teams can support.
