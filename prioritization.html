Institution Types

Three buyer profiles where FraudShield fits naturally.

Digital Banks

Challenger and digital-first banks

Strong fit for institutions managing APP fraud, scam exposure, and customer experience risk in faster payment environments.

  • Typical need: better scam detection without more friction
  • Common buyer set: fraud, ops, risk, and technology leaders
  • Good fit for pilot-led deployment and fast commercial decisions
PSPs and Fintechs

Payment service providers and platforms

Strong fit for teams balancing fraud protection, transaction approval rates, operational cost, and merchant trust.

  • Typical need: lower false positives at high transaction volume
  • Common buyer set: payment risk, fraud ops, and product teams
  • Well suited to API-driven integration patterns
Enterprise Institutions

Regional and Tier 1 banks

Strong fit where executive visibility, regulatory pressure, and implementation credibility matter as much as raw model performance.

  • Typical need: stronger fraud control with governance support
  • Common buyer set: fraud, procurement, security, and risk leaders
  • Often best approached through pilot plus SI-supported rollout

Why these markets matter commercially

Both markets combine concrete reimbursement or scam-prevention pressure with enough operating readiness to make pilot-to-production conversations realistic now.

Engagement Models

Different markets need different commercial motions.

Direct Deployment

UK and Singapore

These regions have the strongest overlap between buyer urgency, implementation speed, and concrete fraud-control need.

  • Best fit for direct pilot and production discussions
  • Strongest buyer narratives for near-term commercial traction
  • Fastest route to measurable customer value
Regional Expansion

Australia and Brazil

Both regions are relevant, but customer conversations are strongest when accompanied by a structured regional or partner-led plan.

  • Australia benefits from UK-style messaging and operating similarity
  • Brazil requires stronger localization and delivery planning
  • Good fit for customers with broader cross-market ambitions
Strategic Programs

Germany, India, and France

These are important long-term markets, but they generally require partner support, staged compliance work, or more customized delivery.

  • Good fit for strategic enterprise conversations
  • Often better positioned with SI or channel support
  • Best discussed after core pilot and deployment alignment
Why the UK and Singapore stand out

In both markets, liability pressure turns fraud prevention from a “nice-to-have” technology discussion into a concrete operating and financial issue. That makes the business case easier to defend across fraud, risk, and finance stakeholders and the implementation discussion more urgent.

Best next conversation

Start with the payment flow and buyer pain, not the full region roadmap.

The strongest customer conversations focus on the institution’s fraud problem first: APP scam exposure, analyst workload, payment speed, reimbursement risk, and rollout constraints. Region matters, but the use case lands first.
